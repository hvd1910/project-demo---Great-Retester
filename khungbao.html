Zelensky: Russia has enough large-scale assault missiles

Saturday, December 17, 2022, 08:59 (GMT+7)

A house suspected of being damaged by a Russian missile in Kryvyi Rih, Ukraine on December 16. Photo: Reuters.

President Zelensky said that Russia has enough missiles to carry out many large-scale raids against Ukraine, but cannot change the balance of power.

"They still have enough missiles for such large-scale attacks," Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky said on December 16, referring to the Russian raid earlier the same day. "Despite what the rocket cultists in Moscow might expect, they have not changed the balance of power in this conflict."


General Valeriy Zaluzhny, commander of the Ukrainian army, said that Russia on December 16 launched 76 missiles from the Caspian and Black Seas at targets throughout the country.

Of the 76 missiles, 72 are Kalibr and Kh-22 cruise missiles, the remaining four are Kh-59 guided-aircraft missiles and Kh-31P anti-radar missiles. General Zaluzhny announced that Ukraine's air defense shot down 60 Russian missiles.

The Russian Defense Ministry has not commented on the latest missile strike against Ukraine.

Ukrainian officials said critical infrastructure in the capital Kiev, Kryvyi Rih province in the center, Kharkov province in the northeast, Sumy in the north and Odessa in southern Ukraine were hit by rockets.

Ukraine's Energy Minister Herman Halushchenko announced that nine power generation facilities and many substations were damaged, and production at nuclear power plants was restricted.

"The energy situation in Kharkov will be very difficult," Halushchenko said. Mayor of Kharkov Ihor Terekhov said the entire city lost power after the Russian raid.

Russian forces have recently increased the use of missiles and suicide drones to attack military command infrastructure, energy and ammunition depots in Ukraine. The raids damaged nearly half of Ukraine's energy infrastructure, left millions without power and disrupted rail transport.
